A tirth is a "ford" — a crossing place. These are the holiest sites where Tirthankaras attained moksha, where great temples rose, and where centuries of pilgrims have walked. Visiting them with devotion is believed to shed deep karma.

Shatrunjay — Palitana
शत्रुंजय महातीर्थ
Palitana, Gujarat
Adinath~3,500 steps860+ temples
The holiest of all Shvetambar tirths — an entire mountain of marble temples. Adinath is said to have visited the hill 93 times, and countless souls have attained moksha here. The climb of roughly 3,500 steps to the Adinath temple at the summit is the pilgrimage of a lifetime; many vow to do the "99 yatra" — 99 ascents. The hill is so sacred that no one may eat, sleep or stay atop it overnight.
Sammed Shikharji
सम्मेद शिखरजी
Parasnath Hill, Jharkhand
20 TirthankarasMoksha bhoomi~27 km trek
The greatest moksha-bhoomi: twenty of the twenty-four Tirthankaras attained liberation on this hill, including Parshvanath, after whom the peak is named. Pilgrims of both Shvetambar and Digambar traditions complete a ~27 km barefoot circuit venerating the tonks (shrines) of each Tirthankara. Many consider one yatra here the fulfilment of a lifetime's wish.
Girnar
गिरनार तीर्थ
Junagadh, Gujarat
Neminath~9,999 steps
The mountain where Neminath, the 22nd Tirthankara, took diksha, attained Kevalgyan, and reached moksha — all three kalyanaks on one mountain. The climb of nearly ten thousand steps passes magnificent temple clusters. It was here that Neminath renounced on his wedding day after hearing the cries of animals gathered for the feast.
Pavapuri
पावापुरी
Nalanda district, Bihar
MahavirNirvana siteJal Mandir
Where Bhagwan Mahavir delivered his final sermon and attained nirvana on Diwali night, 527 BCE. The exquisite Jal Mandir (water temple) floats in the middle of a lotus lake, marking the cremation site — so many devotees took the sacred ash that the ground became a pond. The most sacred place on earth for the memory of Mahavir.
Ranakpur
राणकपुर तीर्थ
Pali district, Rajasthan
Adinath1,444 pillars15th century
The Chaumukha (four-faced) temple of Adinath — one of the most stunning structures ever built in marble. Its 1,444 intricately carved pillars are each unique; no two are alike, and the play of light through them changes by the hour. Built in the 15th century after a minister named Dharna Shah saw the celestial vehicle Nalinigulma in a dream.
Dilwara Temples
देलवाड़ा मंदिर
Mount Abu, Rajasthan
Adinath & NeminathMarble carving11th–13th century
Five temples whose marble carving is considered the finest in the world — ceilings, pillars and doorways carved so finely the stone appears translucent. The Vimal Vasahi (Adinath) and Luna Vasahi (Neminath) temples took generations of artisans, who were famously paid in gold equal to the weight of the marble dust they carved away.
Shankheshwar
शंखेश्वर तीर्थ
Patan district, Gujarat
ParshvanathMiraculous idol
Home of the most revered Parshvanath idol in the Shvetambar world, said to be of immense antiquity — worshipped by Krishna himself according to tradition, and revealed to end an epidemic in his army. Millions of devotees recite "Om Shri Shankheshwar Parshvanathaya Namah" daily. Pilgrims flock here especially on Posh Dashami, Parshvanath's birth kalyanak.
Shravanabelagola
श्रवणबेलगोला
Hassan district, Karnataka
Bahubali57-ft monolithMahamastakabhisheka
The 57-foot monolithic statue of Bahubali (Gommateshwara), carved from a single granite rock in 981 CE, stands atop Vindhyagiri hill — the largest monolithic statue in the world. Bahubali, son of Adinath, stood in meditation so long that vines grew around his legs. Every 12 years the spectacular Mahamastakabhisheka anointing draws millions. The most sacred Digambar tirth.
Champapuri
चंपापुरी
Bhagalpur, Bihar
VasupujyaAll 5 kalyanaks
The only place on earth where all five kalyanaks (auspicious life events) of a Tirthankara occurred — Vasupujya, the 12th Tirthankara, was conceived, born, renounced, attained Kevalgyan and reached moksha all at Champapuri.
Mahudi
महुडी तीर्थ
Gandhinagar district, Gujarat
Ghantakarna MahavirPadmaprabhaSukhadi prasad
Famous for the shrine of Ghantakarna Mahavir, the protective yaksha deva, consecrated by Acharya Buddhisagarsuri in 1923 alongside the main Padmaprabha temple. Its unique tradition: the sukhadi offered as prasad must be eaten within the temple grounds and never taken away. Devotees come to pray for protection and the removal of obstacles.
Ashtapad
अष्टापद
Himalayas (location lost)
AdinathLost tirth
The legendary mountain — identified by many with Mount Kailash — where Adinath, the first Tirthankara, attained moksha. His son, Emperor Bharat, built a jewelled palace of shrines there. Scriptures say only those with great spiritual power can reach it; its earthly location remains unknown, making it the "lost tirth" that pilgrims honor through its replicas in temples across India.
